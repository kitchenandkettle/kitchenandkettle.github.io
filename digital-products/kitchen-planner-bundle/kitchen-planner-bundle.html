How this works

Type into any field. Everything saves automatically to your browser — close the page and come back, your data is still here. Nothing leaves your computer. No account. No signup.

Print these templates and fill them out by hand. Keep them on the fridge, in a binder, or wherever you actually look. Reprint as often as you need.

Tips

  • Your data stays on this computer, in this browser. It won't sync to your phone.
  • Use Print / Save PDF to print blank copies or save a filled copy.
  • The "Clear all data" button wipes everything. Use it when starting a fresh week.
  • Works offline — no internet needed after the first load.
  • Fill out once a week — about 20 minutes. The payoff is not standing in front of the fridge at 5:30pm with no plan.

Grocery List

Before you go: check the pantry and freezer for these items first. Don't buy what you already have.

Pantry Inventory

Reference sheet. Update when something big changes — not every time you use half an onion.

Mark items "L" in the notes column when running low. Cross off (clear the row) when you use the last of something.

Freezer Inventory

The freezer is where food goes to be forgotten. Track what you add and remove.

Common items: stock/broth, cooked beans, frozen veg, meat, bread, dough, cheese, herbs in oil, tomato paste, fruit, leftovers.
Keep this open while you cook. Add items when you freeze them. Cross off when you use them.

Weekly Prep Checklist

The things that make the week easier if you do them ahead. Aim for 3–5.

    The person who prepped three things and actually used them had a better week than the person who prepped ten and burned out.

    A note on systems

    This bundle is a tool, not a standard. Use what helps. Ignore what doesn't. The goal is to reduce the number of decisions you make when you're tired, hungry, and standing in front of an open fridge at the end of a long day.
