## Description (copy-paste)
The point of preserving food is to eat it later. This logbook makes sure "later" actually happens — and that you remember what worked so you can do it again.

Every preserver learns the hard way: you open a jar of tomatoes in February and they're incredible, but you have no idea which batch it was. Or you make pickles that are too soft and wish you'd written down the brine ratio. This logbook solves that.

**What's inside (7 pages):**
- **Canning log** — Two full pages. Date, recipe, jar size, water bath or pressure canner, processing time, yield, and the most important field: how it turned out when you opened it
- **Fermentation tracker** — Start date, ingredients, salt ratio, and tasting notes over time. Ferments change — track the journey
- **Dehydrating notes** — What you dried, prep method, temperature, time, and whether it was worth repeating
- **Seasonal produce guide** — What's in season when, and which preservation method suits it best
- **Year-end review** — Total jars, best batch, worst batch, what to change next year
- **Notes pages** — Blank spaces for recipes to try, lessons learned, and next year's plan

**Two formats included:**
- **Interactive HTML** — Type directly into the logbook. Your entries save automatically to your device (no account, no cloud, fully private). Note: clearing browser cookies or history will clear your entries.
- **Printable PDF** — Print blank copies and fill by hand. Print as many as you need — one per year, or one per method.

**Includes a food safety note:** This logbook assumes you already know safe preserving practices. Consult tested recipes and USDA/NCHFP guidelines before canning.
