Why fewer products?

Most of what a house needs for cleaning can be handled with three things: soap, an acid, and a mild abrasive. The rest is knowing when to use each one — and when a commercial product does the job better.

This isn't about avoiding "chemicals." Everything is chemicals. It's about fewer bottles under your sink, understanding what's in them, and spending less money.

The seven-ingredient cleaning pantry

White vinegar

A mild acid. Dissolves mineral deposits, cuts grease, deodorizes.

Glass, counters, bathroom, laundry rinse, coffee maker.

Honest note

Does not disinfect. Use for cleaning (removing dirt), not sanitizing. If someone is sick, use something stronger.

Baking soda

A mild abrasive and deodorizer. Scrubs without scratching.

Sinks, tubs, stovetops, oven, deodorizing, laundry booster.

Honest note

The baking soda + vinegar fizz is not what cleans — it's just the two neutralizing each other. Scrub with baking soda, rinse, then wipe with vinegar. Don't mix them at the same time.

Castile soap (liquid) or plain bar soap

A true soap from plant oils or gentle fats. Cuts through dirt and grease. Liquid castile is convenient; a plain bar like Ivory or Dove Sensitive works just as well — grate and dissolve in warm water, or rub a damp cloth on the bar.

All-purpose spray, dish soap, hand soap, floors, laundry (soft water).

Honest note

Castile can irritate some people's skin. If it bothers you, switch to a plain unscented bar (Ivory, Dove Sensitive). Cleans just as well and many find it gentler. Don't mix any soap with vinegar — they cancel out.

Citric acid

A stronger, odorless acid. Excellent descaler.

Dishwasher, washing machine, kettle, bathroom fixtures.

Salt (coarse)

Abrasive and absorbent. Good for stubborn residue.

Cast iron, cutting boards, oven spills, wine stains.

Hydrogen peroxide (3%)

Oxidizing cleaner and mild disinfectant.

Stain removal, cutting board sanitizing, grout, laundry.

Honest note

Keep in dark bottle. Don't mix with vinegar (creates irritant).

When to use commercial products

Toilet bowl cleaner, laundry detergent in hard water, dishwasher detergent, disinfectant when sick. This isn't absolutism.

Cleaning Recipes

All-Purpose Spray

Counters, tables, sinks, most hard surfaces. Daily use.

1 cup water • 1 cup white vinegar • optional: 10 drops essential oil

  1. Combine in a spray bottle. Shake before each use.
  2. Spray surface. Wipe with a clean cloth.

Do not use on marble, granite, or natural stone.

Soft Scrub (sinks, tubs, stovetops)

Baking soda • liquid soap or plain bar (castile, Ivory, or Dove) • water

  1. Sprinkle baking soda onto the wet surface.
  2. Add a few drops of liquid soap, or rub a damp cloth on a bar of mild soap.
  3. Scrub with damp cloth. Rinse thoroughly.

Glass & Mirror Cleaner

½ cup isopropyl alcohol (70% or 50%) • ½ cup water

  1. Combine in spray bottle. Shake gently.
  2. Spray onto glass. Wipe with microfiber cloth or newspaper.

Alcohol cuts grease and evaporates fast — zero streaks. No alcohol? ¼ cup vinegar + 1 cup water + 1 tsp cornstarch works too.

Floor Cleaner

1 gallon hot water • ¼ cup white vinegar • 2–3 drops mild liquid soap (optional)

  1. Combine in bucket. Mop as usual.
  2. No need to rinse — vinegar residue evaporates.

Oven Cleaner (non-toxic, requires elbow grease)

Baking soda • water • white vinegar (for final wipe)

  1. Make a paste of baking soda and water. Spread over surfaces.
  2. Let sit overnight (at least 8 hours).
  3. Scrub with damp cloth. Spray vinegar on stubborn spots.

        Laundry & Simple Care

        Laundry that lasts

        • Cold water for most things. Warm for towels and bedding.
        • Less detergent than the bottle says. Start with half.
        • Air dry when you can. The dryer is the hardest thing you do to your clothes.
        • Don't overwash. Jeans, sweaters, outer layers — hang to air out between wears.

        A note on homekeeping

        A clean house is not a moral achievement. A messy house is not a personal failure. These are just systems for making a space that supports your life.

        If you try one thing, make the all-purpose spray. Thirty seconds to mix, costs almost nothing, replaces three bottles under your sink.
