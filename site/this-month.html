A seasonal rhythm

July

The garden is giving and the kitchen is busy. This is the month of abundance.

What's in season

Zucchini, beans, tomatoes, basil, blueberries, raspberries. Garlic scapes are curling — bulbs soon.

What to preserve now

Berries. Zucchini. Cucumbers. Green beans. And if you have hens, they're laying heavily — preserving eggs now means eggs in January when they stop.

Kitchen rhythm

Cook once, eat twice. Grill extra vegetables for tomorrow's lunch. A batch of pesto in the freezer now tastes like July all winter. Skip the oven — stovetop, grill, and no-cook meals keep the kitchen livable.

Household

Window-washing weather. Overcast day, two windows at a time. And if your cleaning system feels like a chore you're avoiding, simplify it.

Good guides for this moment

🌱 The Five Easiest Crops — if your zucchini is out of control, you're winning.

🥚 Egg Preservation — freeze and lime the spring glut.

🧹 Simple Cleaning — systems that hold up when you'd rather be outside.
