Food that lasts

Preservation isn't a hobby or an aesthetic. It's a kitchen skill that extends the life of good food so you can cook with what you have, when you have it.

Fermentation

Vegetables, dairy, beverages, sourdough. The oldest preservation method, still the most alive.

Water bath canning

Jams, pickles, chutneys, tomatoes. Safe, straightforward, satisfying.

Dehydration

Herbs, fruit, mushrooms, jerky. Concentrates flavor, takes almost no storage space.

Root cellaring

Cool, dark, humid. No equipment required beyond knowing which corner of your home stays cold.

Infusions

Vinegars, oils, extracts, bitters, liqueurs. The easiest preservation method. Almost impossible to mess up.

Freezing strategies

Not everything freezes well. Here's what does, how to package it, and how to actually use what you've frozen.
