A seasonal rhythm

July

The garden is giving and the kitchen is busy. This is the month of abundance.

What's in season now

Zucchini is producing faster than you can pick it. Beans are coming in. The first tomatoes are ripening. Basil is lush and needs cutting back regularly. Berries are at peak — strawberries are finishing, blueberries and raspberries are heavy on the bush. Garlic scapes are curling, a sign the bulbs will be ready soon.

This is the month when a garden tips from "I hope something grows" to "what do I do with all of this."

Preservation tasks

Freeze berries flat on a baking sheet, then bag them — they won't clump together and you can pull out a handful at a time for smoothies or oatmeal all winter. Shred and freeze zucchini in 2-cup portions for bread and fritters. Make refrigerator pickles with cucumbers and green beans — no canning needed, just a jar of brine in the fridge.

If you have backyard hens, they're laying heavily right now. This is the moment to preserve eggs for the winter lull — see the water glassing method for the simplest approach.

Kitchen rhythm

Cook once, eat twice. Grill extra vegetables with dinner and they become tomorrow's frittata or grain bowl. A big batch of basil pesto freezes in ice cube trays — pull one out in January and it tastes like July. The oven is not your friend this month. Stovetop, grill, and no-cook meals keep the kitchen bearable.

Household note

Wash windows on an overcast day. Sunlight dries the cleaner too fast and leaves streaks. Overcast gives you time to wipe. This is a two-window-a-week task, not a Saturday project — start with the ones you look through most.
