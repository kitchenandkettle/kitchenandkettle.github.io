Palette Rules

Do

Use Paper or Pantry Cream for all backgrounds. Limit to 3 colors per page. Use color to organize, not decorate.

Don't

Never use #FFFFFF or #000000. Never use more than one accent color for a single element. No gradients, no neon.

Typography Rules

Do

Crimson Pro for anything meant to be read. Work Sans for anything meant to be scanned. Generous line-height, generous whitespace.

Don't

No third typeface. No decorative/display fonts. No all-caps body text. No condensed or extended variants.

Brand Marks

Wordmark & Kettle

The wordmark is the primary logo. The kettle is a supporting brand mark — never a replacement, always a companion.

Kettle Mark

Primary

Charcoal on Paper
Default brand mark for light backgrounds

Reversed

Charcoal kettle inverted for dark backgrounds

Standalone (transparent) — for placement over photography or non-standard backgrounds

Wordmark

Kitchen & Kettle

Crimson Pro 600. Always paired with the tagline or kettle mark — never solo on a blank page.

Clear Space & Minimum Size

Minimum clear space around the wordmark equals the height of the capital "K" in "Keeping." The kettle mark follows the same rule. Neither mark should appear smaller than 1 inch wide in print or 120px wide on screen.

Usage Rules

Do

Use the Charcoal kettle as your default. Pair it with the wordmark on covers and headers. Let it sit small and calm — it's an heirloom, not a badge.

Don't

No stretching, rotating, or recoloring outside the palette. No drop shadows or outlines. The kettle is never a replacement for the wordmark — it supports, it doesn't stand alone.

Voice

Brand Voice

Talk like the most competent person you know, who is also the kindest.

DimensionGuideline
ToneWarm, steady, calm. Not chipper. Not dry.
AuthorityEarned, not claimed. "This works because I've done it."
ComplexityClear enough for beginners, never condescending to experts.
HumorDry, occasional, never at anyone's expense.
EmotionHonest but contained. Acknowledge hard things, then offer something useful.
GenderNeutral by default. Skills belong to everyone.

Examples

✗ "Revolutionize your morning routine with these 5 game-changing hacks!"

✓ "Here's a breakfast system that works. It's not exciting, but it holds up on mornings when you can't think."

✗ "DITCH the toxic chemicals! Your family deserves better!"

✓ "Vinegar and soap handle most household cleaning. Here's when to use each, and when you actually need something stronger."

✗ "Slow living in our farmhouse kitchen 🕯️🌿"

✓ "This bread recipe takes most of an afternoon, but most of that is waiting. The actual work is about twenty minutes."

Tagline

Works because it works.

Core philosophy distilled into five words. Use as primary tagline on all materials.
